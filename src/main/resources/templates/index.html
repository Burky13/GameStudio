List of games:

  • Minesweeper
    Minesweeper is a single-player puzzle video game. The objective of the game is to clear a rectangular board containing hidden "mines" or bombs without detonating any of them, with help from clues about the number of neighboring mines in each field. The game originates from the 1960s.

  • Puzzle
    The 15-puzzle (also called Gem Puzzle, Boss Puzzle, Game of Fifteen, Mystic Square and many others) is a sliding puzzle that consists of a frame of numbered square tiles in random order with one tile missing. The puzzle also exists in other sizes, particularly the smaller 8-puzzle. If the size is 3 by 3 tiles, the puzzle is called the 8-puzzle or 9-puzzle, and if 4 by 4 tiles, the puzzle is called the 15-puzzle or 16-puzzle named, respectively, for the number of tiles and the number of spaces. The object of the puzzle is to place the tiles in order by making sliding moves that use the empty space.

  • Guess a Number
    Guess The Number is a game where you must use your logic in order to guess a 4-digit secret number selected by the computer at the beginning of the game. The number is formed with digits from 0 to 100 or 1000 or 10 000 depending on the choosed difficulty. Using information from the computer's reply, you must guess the number using as few moves as possible. Good luck!

  • Tic Tac Toe
    Tic-tac-toe (also known as noughts and crosses or Xs and Os) is a paper-and-pencil game for two players, X and O, who take turns marking the spaces in the grid. The player who succeeds in placing five of their marks in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row wins the game. Tic-Tac -Toe involves looking ahead and trying to figure out what the person playing against you might do next.
